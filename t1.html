Whether it's part of your inter-railing gap yahh or just as part of a weekend city break; ​the small city of Lübeck in Northern Germany is WELL worth your visit this Christmas. 

How to get there

Located in Northern Germany, Hamburg is the closest airport to fly into. From there, you can get the train to Lübeck. They run hourly and only take about 40 mins, so it's pretty simple.

Where to stay

Lübeck runs along the Baltic coast, but to get a real Christmassy feel of the city, we reckon it's best to stay within the city's gates. The Old Town (or the Alstadt) is surrounded by water on all sides and there are loads of places to stay - to suit whatever your budget. The Radisson Blu on the water is gorgeous and close to the Holstentor city gate.  If you're feeling fancy there's Hotel Anno 1216 which has traditional German rooms (apparently the Swedish King once stayed) or if you're doing it on the cheap - Air BnB has great budget friendly rooms.​

What to do

There's LOADS to do to really fill your time with in Lübeck. Known as 'the marzipan capital of the world' a trip to the world famous Niederegger Cafe is a MUST. Check out the amazing hand crafted marzipan treats, learn about how it's made in the museum and obviously, eat your own weight in marzipan and cakes​ upstairs at the ​Niederegger Cafe​.

If you're into history, then a visit of the museums is definitely one to do - from the ​Buddenbrookhaus to ​Günter Grass-Haus museums, Lübeck is home to three Noble Prize winners and famously rejected Hitler in the second world war. Plus the ​Holstentor​ city gates are printed on the back of a 2 piece Euro. FUN FACT. 

[image id="50858" caption="" loc="C" share="true" expand="true"]

The cobbled old city is housed within seven Church steeples. Walk to the top of one of these, admire the views and get your instagram fill.

Have dinner at the Schiffergesellschaft restaurant which has been housed in the same historic building for the last 480 years, and has been a restaurant for the public since 1866. Decorated with historic maritime artefacts and huuuuge ship models- it's a delicious and truly authentic traditional German restaurant. 

OBVIOUSLY a trip to visit the Christmas markets is a must. Found in the city's centre, the markets capture a seriously magical German christmas atmosphere. Eat spit roasted spiced ham sliced thickly on warm crusty bread, explore the traditional cobbled streets, stock up on wooden christmas souvenirs, eat even more marzipan and basically embrace your inner Buddy the Elf .
